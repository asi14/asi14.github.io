Hobbies are a major part of life, and gives it much of if its value. These are the hobbies/activities I cherish.

Hobbies
Computer Science: the usage of computers to solve/answer problems. Computer Science is used in a lot of different ways- both to create software and to help scientists offer answers to real-world problems.
Involvements: I am involved in a Computer Science class, and plan to take the AP Computer Science next year. I also go to hackathons, where I spend an intensive amount of time (e.g. 2 days) programming straight through the day and night. It's a fun thing to do.
Volunteering: an activity where people willingly give up their time and expenses for a greater cause. Generally, this is for a charitable cause (e.g. helping those in need), and offers benefits to the reciever and giver. Volunteering takes up many shapes and forms, both outside and inside, and can often be a life-changing experience.
Involvements: I am involved with my school's Key Club, part of an international club called Key Club International, which is dedicated to volunteering. We go to various places and volunteer for charitable causes. I also volunteer at my local library, where I help move books wherever necessary.

 Railfanning: a passionate interest in trains. A global movement, railfans are at the forefront of the train preservation effort. Often, railfans make communities dedicated to special trains (e.g. the Norfolk & Western No. 611) which are intimately known within both the railfan and general community.
Involvements: I'm a passionate railfan of the NYC Subway. I've spent many nights reading about the Subway, watching railfan videos, etc. I also try to go on railfan trips, where the MTA brings out vintage trains to temporary service (e.g. the Train of Many Colors). If I'm lucky, I'll be able to intern with the MTA this summer.

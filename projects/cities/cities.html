Popular Cities

The Empire State Building, a landmark of New York City.

Credits: Wikipedia

The city has become the predominant mode of living all throughout the world, both in its urban and in its suburban aspects. Plenty of cities are big and famous, but a select few have emerged to be widely known and visited by tourists from all around the world. This list is a compilation of the best cities have to offer.

New York City, New York

Of the world's biggest cities, most people think of New York City on the spot. "The City that Never Sleeps", New York is a thriving cultural and economic center that attracts millions of (usually unwanted (by its inhabitants)) tourists every year. New York City has plenty of notable landmarks, such as the Empire State Building, the Statue of Liberty, and Grand Central Terminal, one of the busiest (and most elaborately built) terminals in the world.

Boston, Massachussets

Boston, Massachussets: one of the oldest cities in America. It played host to a number of key events of the American Revolution (e.g. the Boston Tea Party), and has artifacts from nearly all the United State's major time periods. Today is a major center of education and culture. Its subway, the Massachussetts Bay Transportation Authority, is the oldest country in America, and immediately suceeded an older transit authority called the Metropolitan Transit Authority (Coincidence? I think not!).

Seoul, Korea

Seoul, Korea: the capital and largest city of South Korea. It has a long history stetching back nearly 2,000 years and has a rich cultural past. Today, it is home to lots of landmarks, is a major technology, economic, and tourism hub, and is a major supporter of the United States. It is especially known for being the birthplace of K-Pop, a major musical movement that has taken the world by storm with its unique blend of English and Korean to create something altogether new and unprecedented in music's long history.
